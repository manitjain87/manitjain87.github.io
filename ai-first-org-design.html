AI-First Org Design and its impact on cost structure

January 15, 2026 · 9 min read

Key insight from 2026 Anthropic AI job impact report: AI is already capable of doing 94% of jobs in the "computer and maths" category, but current actual coverage is only 33%. There's a massive gap between what AI could do and what is actually being done.

The Thought Experiment

If you built a $10Bn+ e-commerce shop from scratch today, how would you structure the workforce and agents across functions? This requires fundamentally rethinking how work gets done.

Instead of a traditional pyramid structure heavily reliant on human capital for execution, this new org resembles an hourglass:

  • Small group of strategic directors at the top
  • AI agents and automated systems forming the massive middle layer of execution
  • Highly specialized human layer managing exceptions, physical operations, and oversight

The Numbers

Revenue~$12 Billion
SG&A / Overhead$2B+
Headcount12,000 employees (60% reduction vs. traditional org)

Function-by-Function Breakdown

Engineering

AI agents write/test/deploy code. Elite "Orchestrators" replace junior devs, shifting the human role from creator to editor.

Customer Support

AI resolves 95% of Tier 1/2 inquiries. Remaining humans act as "Empathy Escalation Specialists" for high-value VIP issues.

Catalog & Merchandising

Multimodal AI ingests catalogs and generates 3D/SEO assets instantly. Human roles become purely strategic trend forecasters.

Corporate SG&A (HR, Legal, Finance, Commercial)

A tiny "BizOps" team runs the company using AI simulations.

Logistics & Warehouse (The Physical Moat)

AI optimizes routing, but physical labor remains; robotics is evolving fast though.

The Economic Impact

Revenue per employee increases 2-2.5X, decoupling growth from headcount. An AI-first architecture allows you to reinvest into the core pillars of e-commerce, creating a flywheel effect. It allows you to slash prices for customers and give them a "magical" experience.

As product discovery shifts from search engines (Google) and social feeds (Meta) to conversational commerce (AI assistants like Gemini, ChatGPT), the traditional performance marketing playbook breaks too.

Open Questions

  • Can incumbents pivot? Against the massive friction of "how we've always done it"? How to re-structure incentives?
  • Creativity unleashed? Today's structures in large orgs kill creativity — as AI agents take over the mundane and repetitive, does it get unleashed?
  • OPEX shift: You're trading human OPEX for compute OPEX. How to forecast this massive compute costs?
  • Security: New security concerns with AI-driven execution?
